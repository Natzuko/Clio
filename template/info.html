CLIO ELECTRONIC ART GALLERY

art, science, and code

CLIO is the first electronic art gallery specializing in integrating home and building automation, focusing on interactive works. It aims to offer unique and immersive experiences and integrate art, technology, and intelligent design.

Two types of works are offered: automatization works and interactive works. Home automation works can be connected to home automation applications via internal network or Wi-Fi, allowing complete integration with the rest of the automation systems. The interactive works are autonomous and operate independently with a simple on/off switch.

In Argentina, there are currently four companies dedicated to home automation and building automation, in Forbes Argentina magazine they project an expansion of 41%. There are various applications to connect subsystems in the home today.

Clio's mission is to promote creativity and technological advancement. The marketing circuit must ensure the correct installation of the work and its maintenance. Provide a 1-year performance warranty (tentative period). And offer a periodic maintenance system. Which may or may not be included in the price of the work. Another distinctive sign of Clío is that we will work with commissioned works.

What home and building automation technology will be used in Clio and how will it be integrated into the artworks?

We will use hybrid automation systems such as KNX, Z-Wave, and Zigbee that integrate perfectly with other lighting, multimedia, etc. controls. Which will allow us to interact with the motion, temperature, and sound sensors, and thus generate interactive responses. They are hybrid systems (wired/wi-fi)

How do we ensure interoperability between different home automation devices and platforms?

We will use integration platforms such as Home Assistant or OpenHAB that allow communication between devices from different manufacturers and protocols. This will ensure a seamless and cohesive experience across all facilities.

What is the financing plan for Clio?

For Clio, two business models were considered with and without financing.

The first business model, without financing, is based on a collaborative system between artists, installation and assembly technicians. The initial expenses will be met from the commissioning and/or sale of the works. This slow growth model is developed from the addition of partners, mentoring for SMEs focused on technological innovation, subsidies, and sponsorships from technology companies.

The second business model, with initial financing, projects gradual growth in revenue as we increase our visibility and expand. How do you plan to manage operating costs? The idea is to keep operating costs low and hire essential personnel for each stage of the gallery's development.

What would the gallery-artist relationship be like?

The artists who make up the gallery must provide the technical sheet of the work and the code. It is necessary to create a database. The use of Collective Access is suggested, it is an open-source platform, developed specifically for the management of art collections of galleries, museums, etc. It is necessary to make a representation contract with the artists involved. The technical documentation of each work is an essential requirement. The aforementioned contract will establish commissions, image rights, and rights to disseminate the work. Periods of validity of the contract, value of the work, etc.

What types of technologies are currently used in electronic arts?

The development of the works in their entirety is carried out with open-source systems, in sound and visual arts. They are systems that dialogue with each other and have low software requirements. Even works of generative art. In small and medium-scale works, production costs are relatively low. This group belongs to those that have motion sensors, temperature, infrared and ultraviolet sensors, magnets, etc. Large-scale works, or mega-installations of course have a higher cost for obvious reasons, wiring, etc. These types of installations may be part of the gallery in the next stage of development and expansion.

The sensors and different devices in Argentina are low-cost. There is an artistic field in which this type of work is currently being carried out, it is just that they are not nucleated and the majority apply to scholarships or artistic residencies.

Clio aspires to become an international benchmark in the field of electronic arts, due to its innovative approach. We want to inspire artists, engineers, and the general public to explore new ways of interacting with the environment through advanced technologies.

Clio proposes working in collaborative environments and creating a community between artists, designers, engineers, and universities, through events, streamings, and educational workshops, to promote knowledge and the development of skills in creative spaces.

When Clio is already positioned in the market, it will be able to develop the following areas:

Exhibition Rooms: Areas specifically designed to display works of art that interact with the environment through sensors, actuators, and automated control systems.

Innovation Laboratory: A space dedicated to research and development, where artists and technologists can experiment and create new works using home and building automation tools.

Educational Center: Facilities for workshops, courses, and conferences, focused on education about electronic art and automation technologies.

Interactive Spaces: Areas where visitors can experience first-hand the integration of art and technology in a controlled and intelligent environment.

Conclusion: Clio is not just a gallery, it is a platform for innovation and creative development in permanent dialogue with art and technology to redefine the perceptible limits of the spaces we inhabit.

Clio is much more than a financial and artistic venture, in personal terms, it is a platform for artistic projection for new generations, a structure of support and economic solvency for new future artists, today in full development.
